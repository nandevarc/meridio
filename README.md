# Meridio

Workflow-native system for discovering, qualifying, and managing early-stage opportunities.

Built around high-volume research workflows where discovery, filtering, execution, and tracking happen continuously across multiple stages.

---

## Core Concept

Meridio is designed around the lifecycle of opportunities — from raw discovery to active execution.

Instead of functioning as a simple tracker or database, Meridio focuses on:
- reducing operational friction,
- maintaining execution momentum,
- organizing high-volume discovery,
- and helping manage active opportunities with clearer workflow transitions.

---

## Workflow Architecture

### RAW
High-volume discovery and intake layer.

Used for:
- X/Twitter discoveries,
- CT signals,
- scraping sources,
- ecosystem monitoring,
- and unfiltered early-stage opportunities.

Purpose:
- fast capture,
- lightweight screening,
- quick validation before qualification.

Typical activities:
- signal checking,
- website verification,
- category estimation,
- quick filtering,
- initial relevance assessment.

---

### CURATED
Qualified opportunity layer.

Projects that pass initial filtering move into deeper evaluation and categorization.

Used for:
- Layer 1 Triage,
- Deep Screen,
- Fast Analyst workflow,
- conviction building,
- timing evaluation,
- opportunity qualification.

Purpose:
- decision support,
- structured research,
- prioritization and qualification.

---

### ACTIVE
Execution and operational layer.

Projects that are already considered actionable move into active workflow management.

Used for:
- execution playbooks,
- tracking,
- lifecycle monitoring,
- reminders,
- progress management,
- opportunity continuity.

Purpose:
- maintain execution momentum,
- reduce context switching,
- support long-running active plays.

## Philosophy

Meridio is built from real-world high-volume research workflows rather than generic productivity assumptions.

The goal is not simply storing information, but supporting the full lifecycle of opportunities:
- discovery,
- qualification,
- execution,
- tracking,
- and continuity.
